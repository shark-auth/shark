## Problem Statement
Mexican technology companies and industrial players in Monterrey are facing a double-bind: they must adopt Agentic AI to stay competitive, but they are forced to outsource their identity and security logic to "black-box" US-based SaaS (Auth0, Okta, Clerk). This dependency creates:
1. **Sovereignty Risks:** Critical identity logic and user data residency are managed outside Mexico.
2. **Agentic Gaps:** Traditional auth systems are built for humans with browsers, not autonomous agents that need to delegate authority (Agent A acting on behalf of User X to Agent B).
3. **Complexity Throttling:** Small teams spend weeks DIY-ing "Agent Auth" middleware, often leading to insecure API key leaks and un-traceable delegation chains.

## Demand Evidence
"Agent auth is the new user auth." Developers on Reddit and LinkedIn have confirmed that for real agentic workflows, they are currently forced to "DIY everything like auth in the old days." The user identifies a massive need for a 39MB binary that provides a sovereign open core for this new era.

## Status Quo
- **DIY Middleware:** Teams duct-tape custom headers and manual JWT validation.
- **Insecure Tokens:** High risk of API key leakage because agents handle static secrets.
- **Blind Delegation:** No real way to trace the chain of "who authorized whom" across agentic handoffs.

## Target User & Narrowest Wedge
- **The Human:** The "Lead Dev" or "CTO" at a Monterrey-based logistics or fintech startup who needs to deploy an agentic supply-chain or payment system this week.
- **The Wedge:** **The Sovereign Agent Proxy.** A 39MB self-hostable binary that handles DPoP (Proof-of-Possession) and delegation tracing out of the box, allowing agents to authenticate securely without leaking secrets.

## Constraints
- **Performance:** Must maintain low latency for Monterrey-to-Regional traffic.
- **Residency:** Must be self-hostable in Mexican private clouds or local hardware.
- **Completeness:** Human auth (Passwords, OAuth, Passkeys) must be robust enough to replace legacy systems.

## Premises
1. **Identity is Infrastructure:** Mexico cannot have a sovereign AI future if its identity layer is owned by foreign SaaS.
2. **Agents Need Better Auth:** M2M (Machine-to-Machine) auth is shifting from static keys to dynamic, bound, and delegated tokens (DPoP + Act claims).
3. **The Proxy is the Interface:** Inserting a proxy in front of services is the fastest way to add security without rewriting legacy code.
